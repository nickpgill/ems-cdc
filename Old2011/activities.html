European Mathematical Society
Committee for the Developing Countries
Activities

Aims and Objectives

  • to assist developing countries in all possible ways
  • some examples:
    * mathematics curriculum development
    * cooperation with local staff in conducting M.Sc. and Ph.D. programs
    * helping to build up libraries
    * helping to build up regional centres and networks
    * to provide information about further studies for students from developing regions
    * to mobilize funds for junior and senior researchers to attend conferences

Our activities (past, present and future)

  • book donation scheme: donors and recipients welcome (over 10 tonnes sent)
  • funded participants to ICM06, a speaker to Mongolia, a (part) CIMPA school, conferences, etc.
  • supporting Cambodian students to do M.Sc.
  • roundtable in 5ECM, discussion group at ICM10
  • to hold e-literature workshops (Bamako 11/2010)
  • Twinning project: starting seed grant (~ 500 euros)
  • Emerging Regional Centres of Excellence ERCE

Short Description

    This committee consists of about ten members, most of whom are engaged in development work in their capacity as heads of various such projects, e.g.

  • Centre International de Mathématiques Pures et Appliquées (CIMPA) (France and Spain),
  • The International Science Programme at Uppsala University (ISP) (Sweden),
  • The Interdisciplinary Center for Scientific Computing (IWR)! (Germany),
and are also active in other committees for developing countries around the world, notably within
  • The International Mathematical Union (IMU),
  • International Council for Industrial and Applied Mathematics (ICIAM), ICIAM Conference Support for Applied and Industrial Mathematics in Developing Countries
  • The Abdus Salam International Center for Theoretical Physics (ICTP),
  • The London Mathematical Society (LMS),
  • Hungarian Academy, etc.
In addition to committee members we have associates who help us in our work, in many ways.
    We really started our activities in earnest only 2002, but since then we have expanded them and been involved in quite a number of projects. One important project is our book donation scheme, whereby donations of books and journals from the developed world are sent to departments in the developing world. This is supported financially, on a case-by-case basis, by
    * ICTP,
    * American Mathematical Society (AMS),
    * LMS,
    * Zentralblatt,
    * The Norwegian Programme for Development, Research and Education (NUFU),
and also our own very limited funds. Thus we have moved tens of tonnes of books and journals, from Europe, from USA, from Canada, to Africa, to South East Asia, to South and Central America.
    Two new projects are in the pipeline:
  • Mentoring African Research Mathematics (MARM)
  • twinning of departments between developed and developing regions.

    We also have occasionally funded participants and speakers to conferences, e.g. ICM06 in Madrid, and a conference in Mongolia in 2010.
    We are full of ideas, and there are lots of work to be done. We do not receive regular funding from our parent society EMS, and so desperately need help from all mathematicians!
